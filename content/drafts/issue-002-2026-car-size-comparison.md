# Issue #002: The F1 Diet

**Title:** The F1 Diet: How Much Smaller Are the 2026 Cars?

**Subtitle:** Breaking down the numbers behind F1's biggest size reduction in years

---

## The cars are finally shrinking. Here's by how much.

For years, F1 fans have complained that the cars are too big. "Boats" and "barges" became the go-to insults. Monaco looked ridiculous. Overtaking felt impossible on narrow tracks.

The FIA listened. The 2026 regulations bring the most significant size reduction in modern F1. I've pulled together all the numbers so you can see exactly what's changing.

---

## The Numbers

| Spec | 2025 | 2026 | Change |
|------|------|------|--------|
| **Minimum Weight** | 800kg | 768kg | -32kg |
| **Wheelbase** | 3,600mm | 3,400mm | -200mm |
| **Width** | 2,000mm | 1,900mm | -100mm |
| **Floor Width** | — | — | -150mm |

That's a 4% weight reduction (32kg ÷ 800kg) and 20cm shorter wheelbase. Doesn't sound like much? Let me put it in context.

---

## Why 32kg Matters

Every 10kg of extra weight costs about **0.3 seconds per lap** (a widely cited F1 rule of thumb, varying by circuit).

That means the 32kg reduction alone is worth nearly a full second. Teams at the front and back of the grid could see their gaps shift purely based on who hits the weight target.

Here's the catch: Williams boss James Vowles said most teams will struggle to hit 768kg initially.

> "It would be good to find out from others where they are, but I think most will be overweight. That's the simple facts behind it. It's a very aggressive target."

The teams that nail the weight target early will have a serious advantage.

---

## Shorter, Narrower, More Agile

The 200mm wheelbase reduction means:
- Better turn-in response
- Easier to place on narrow tracks
- Less understeer in tight corners

The 100mm width reduction means:
- More room for side-by-side racing
- Monaco might actually work again
- Easier to defend and attack

The floor is also 150mm narrower, which changes the aerodynamic balance entirely.

---

## The Tyre Factor

Pirelli is also slimming down:
- Front tyres: **25mm narrower**
- Rear tyres: **30mm narrower**
- Weight saving: **~5kg per set**

Narrower tyres mean less mechanical grip but also less drag. Combined with the active aero, straight-line speeds should increase significantly.

---

## What This Means for Racing

**Faster straights:** Up to 40% less drag with active aero in "X-Mode"

**Slower corners:** 15-30% less downforce initially

**Better battles:** Smaller cars are easier to place wheel-to-wheel

**Weight wars:** Teams who miss 768kg will lose nearly a second per lap

## The Bottom Line

F1 cars have been getting bigger and heavier for over a decade. The 2026 regulations finally reverse that trend.

32kg lighter. 20cm shorter. 10cm narrower.

It might not sound revolutionary, but when tenths of a second decide championships, these changes matter. The first team to hit 768kg while maximising performance will have a significant head start.
