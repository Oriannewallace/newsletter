## The Number That Actually Matters

When I worked in wealth management, I learned that sophisticated investors don't just look at rent or price in isolation. They look at **yield** - the return you get relative to what you paid.

There are two types:

**Gross Yield** = Annual Rent √∑ Purchase Price
- Quick and dirty
- Ignores all the costs that eat into your return

**Net Yield** = (Annual Rent - All Expenses) √∑ Purchase Price
- The real number
- What actually hits your bank account

That second one is what matters. And almost nobody calculates it properly.

---

## A Real Example

Let's say you're comparing two properties:

**Property A: Sea Point apartment**
- Purchase price: R2,500,000
- Monthly rent: R18,000
- Gross yield: 8.6% ‚Üê Looks great!

**Property B: Observatory apartment**
- Purchase price: R1,500,000
- Monthly rent: R12,000
- Gross yield: 9.6% ‚Üê Even better!

Property B wins, right?

Not so fast.

When you factor in the actual expenses:

| Expense | Sea Point | Observatory |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| Rates | R2,200/mo | R1,400/mo |
| Levies | R4,500/mo | R1,800/mo |
| Insurance | R600/mo | R400/mo |
| Maintenance | R900/mo | R600/mo |
| **Total** | **R8,200/mo** | **R4,200/mo** |

Now the picture changes:

**Property A Net Yield: 4.7%**
**Property B Net Yield: 6.2%**

Property B still wins - but the gap is different than the gross yield suggested. And that Sea Point "8.6% yield" is actually under 5% when you account for those killer levies.

## What I'd Actually Do

If I was buying a rental property tomorrow (and I think about this constantly), here's my checklist:

1. **Calculate net yield first** - If it's under 5%, the numbers probably don't work
2. **Check the levy history** - Ask for 3 years of levy statements. Are they increasing fast?
3. **Factor in vacancy** - Budget for 1 month empty per year (8% vacancy)
4. **Compare against alternatives** - Could you get 8% in a money market fund with zero hassle?

The property still needs to make sense as an *investment*, not just feel good as a purchase.
